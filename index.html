Ambre Antique

2020

Art Direction

Visual Identity

Product Design

3D Design

Ambre Antique is a fictitious project aimed to challenge the convention of what an oriental perfume can be. The vial is a modern interpretation of black-figure pottery, a technique used in ancient Greece between the 7th - 5th centuries BC, where the figures that covered the ceramics were painted in black against the red body of the vessels. Originally launched by Coty in 1905, 115 years ago, the new iteration of Ambre Antique strives to join the colourful design language of antique Greek vases with a contemporary minimalism to create friction between the two aesthetics.

Material Tales

2020

Art Direction

Visual Identity

UX / UI Design

Visual identity made in collaboration with the second year fashion students at Beckmans College of Design showcasing the creation of magic from materials. The project was realized through a responsive website, a short film, still photography of all 35 looks as well as 13 close-up 3D scans of the materials used to create the different garments. Made in collaboration with Alexander Peri, Alva Nylander, Hedvig Moberg, Joel Eriksson, Linnea Jacobson & Tuva Larsson.

Sequence Paper

2021

Art Direction

Editorial Design

Sequence Paper is a magazine about time and our relationship to it. The magazine features a non-linear narrative that affectionately blends together different perspectives regarding the past, the present and the future. It aims to capture a distinctive yet timeless editorial identity while simultaneously being contemporary. The magazine can be read from both directions in a palindrome-like sequence. Printed at Newspaper Club, London, in uncoated broadsheet format – with an exclusive edition of 50 copies. Featuring poetry from Johan Nordbäck & Antonella Basagni. Made in collaboration with: Alexander Peri, Clémentine Berglund & Joel Eriksson.
